# 06 — Nutrition

## Scope
Fueling strategies, macronutrient needs, micronutrients, and supplementation across the menstrual cycle. ISSN-territory literature.

## What belongs here
- Energy availability and caloric needs across cycle phases
- Macronutrient metabolism differences (carbohydrate oxidation, fat oxidation, protein turnover) across phases
- Iron status, iron deficiency, and menstruation-related iron loss in athletes
- Micronutrients with cycle-phase relevance: magnesium, calcium, vitamin D, B vitamins
- Pre-, intra-, and post-exercise fueling strategies adjusted for cycle phase
- Supplement evidence relevant to female athletes: creatine, caffeine, omega-3, collagen/vitamin C for injury
- Energy intake patterns, cravings, and appetite regulation across the cycle
- Low Energy Availability (LEA) as a nutritional risk (overlap with RED-S → also file in 08)

## What does NOT belong here
- General sports nutrition not specific to sex or cycle (unless foundational)
- RED-S clinical management (→ 08)
- Training adaptation studies where nutrition is not the primary variable (→ 03, 04)

## Priority sources
ISSN Position Stands, ACSM/DC/AND Joint Position Statement on Nutrition and Athletic Performance, IOC consensus statements on female athlete health.

## Inclusion criteria
Human subjects preferred. Animal studies accepted only for mechanistic questions with no human equivalent. Record: population (athlete/non-athlete), phase verification (if applicable), and outcome measures.
