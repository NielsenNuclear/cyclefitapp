# 04 — Endurance Training

## Scope
Aerobic and endurance *adaptation* studies — multi-week training interventions with cardiovascular or metabolic outcomes.

## What belongs here
- Training studies measuring VO₂max, lactate threshold, running economy, or time-trial performance as adaptation outcomes
- Substrate utilisation differences across the cycle with relevance to endurance fueling strategy
- Cardiorespiratory adaptations to endurance training as a function of cycle phase or hormonal status
- Phase-timed endurance periodisation protocols
- Heat acclimatisation and thermoregulatory adaptation studies in female endurance athletes

## What does NOT belong here
- Single-session aerobic performance measures without a training intervention (→ 02)
- Resistance training adaptations (→ 03)
- Nutrition/fueling without training context (→ 06)
- Systematic reviews (→ 09) or meta-analyses (→ 10)

## Inclusion criteria
Multi-week (≥ 4 weeks) endurance training interventions in women of reproductive age. Record: sample size, phase verification method, training modality, outcome measures, effect sizes. Flag studies that do not verify phase hormonally.

## Evidence note
Endurance research in women is less extensive than in men and tends toward smaller samples. Flag underpowered studies explicitly — a null finding in n=8 is not strong evidence of no effect.
