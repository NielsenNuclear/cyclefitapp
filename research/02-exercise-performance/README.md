# 02 — Exercise Performance

## Scope
Acute exercise performance outcomes measured across the menstrual cycle — strength, power, and endurance performance at a single time-point, not training adaptations over time.

## What belongs here
- Cross-sectional or within-subject studies comparing maximal or submaximal performance (1RM, peak power, VO₂max, time-trial) across cycle phases
- Studies measuring perceived exertion, pain threshold, or perceived readiness as a function of cycle phase
- Cardiorespiratory and neuromuscular function across the cycle (acute measures)
- Heat tolerance, substrate utilisation, and ventilatory responses across phases

## What does NOT belong here
- Multi-week training interventions measuring adaptation (→ 03, 04)
- Recovery-specific outcomes (→ 05)
- Hormonal cycle mechanics without a performance outcome (→ 01)

## Inclusion criteria
Human subjects, laboratory or field studies, women of reproductive age. Phase verification method must be reported or inferrable; record it for each entry. Studies without any phase verification (calendar only where LH/serum was feasible) should be flagged as high-misclassification-risk.

## Evidence note
**Design thesis lens:** effect sizes for phase → acute performance are typically small and inconsistent across studies. Note the effect size and verification method for every entry. Distinguish null findings from underpowered studies — they are not the same claim.
