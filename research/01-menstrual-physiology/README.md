# 01 — Menstrual Physiology

## Scope
Hormonal cycle mechanics, phase definitions, and cycle-phase measurement and verification methods.

## What belongs here
- Endocrinology of the menstrual cycle (estrogen, progesterone, LH, FSH, testosterone trajectories)
- Operational definitions of cycle phases (follicular, ovulatory, luteal, menstrual) and the evidence basis for each cutoff
- Cycle length variability and regularity data in athletic and general populations
- Phase-verification methods: calendar-only, urinary LH, serum hormone assay, basal body temperature, ultrasound — and their accuracy/reliability tradeoffs
- Anovulatory cycle literature (prevalence in athletes, impact on hormone exposure)
- Menstrual cycle tracking methods and their validity

## What does NOT belong here
- Performance outcome studies (→ 02)
- Training adaptation studies (→ 03, 04)
- Contraceptive effects on the cycle (→ 07)
- Cycle tracking apps/wearables (→ 12)

## Inclusion criteria
Peer-reviewed primary studies, review articles, and consensus statements that specifically characterise cycle mechanics or phase measurement methodology. Population: women of reproductive age; athletes and non-athletes both accepted here.

## Evidence note
Phase-verification rigor varies enormously across the literature. Flag the verification method for every entry (calendar, LH, serum, BBT, or unstated). Unstated = high risk of phase misclassification bias.
