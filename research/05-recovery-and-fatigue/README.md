# 05 — Recovery and Fatigue

## Scope
Recovery, delayed-onset muscle soreness (DOMS), perceived fatigue, readiness, and sleep as a function of menstrual cycle phase or symptoms.

## What belongs here
- Studies on muscle damage, DOMS severity, and recovery time across cycle phases
- Perceived fatigue, energy, and motivation as reported outcomes across the cycle
- Sleep quality, architecture, and duration across the cycle (polysomnography and self-report)
- Heart rate variability (HRV) and autonomic nervous system responses across phases
- Readiness-to-train perception and self-reported recovery quality
- Inflammatory markers relevant to recovery (CRP, cytokines) across the cycle
- Studies on pain sensitivity and pain modulation across cycle phases

## What does NOT belong here
- Acute performance outcomes (→ 02)
- Training-load-induced fatigue in the absence of cycle context (→ 02 or 03/04)
- RED-S and menstrual dysfunction (→ 08)

## Inclusion criteria
Studies in women of reproductive age with a clearly defined cycle-phase dimension. Self-report instruments must be named (e.g. POMS, RPE, PSQI). Phase verification method recorded for every entry.

## Evidence note
**This is the highest-defensibility domain for Axis.** Evidence that symptoms (pain, fatigue, poor sleep) affect perceived readiness and training behavior is comparatively more consistent than phase → performance evidence. Prioritise this folder for the app's in-product evidence hub.
