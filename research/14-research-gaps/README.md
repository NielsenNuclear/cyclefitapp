# 14 — Research Gaps

## Scope
Explicitly flagged research gaps, calls for future research, stated study limitations, and documented limitations of the current evidence base as a whole.

## What belongs here
- "Future directions" and "limitations" sections from primary papers, extracted and filed here when they identify a systemic gap (not just limitations of one paper)
- Review papers whose primary contribution is mapping what is *not* yet known
- Editorial and commentary pieces calling for new research directions
- Documented methodological weaknesses in the menstrual cycle + exercise literature as a whole (e.g. overreliance on calendar-only phase verification, small samples, homogeneous populations, lack of OCP disclosure)
- Areas where Axis makes design decisions with insufficient evidence — these must be documented here and flagged in the app's evidence hub

## What does NOT belong here
- Primary studies that happen to mention limitations (record limitations as metadata in the source entry, not as a separate item here unless the gap claim is the paper's main contribution)

## Inclusion criteria
Any identified gap that is relevant to Axis's design decisions. No minimum evidence level required — this folder exists precisely for the places where strong evidence does not yet exist.

## Required entries (to be filed when available)
- [ ] A summary of phase-verification quality across the resistance training literature
- [ ] A note on the underrepresentation of elite and competitive athletes in cycle research
- [ ] A note on the near-total absence of data on transgender and non-binary athletes
- [ ] A note on population homogeneity: most studies are white, Western, university-aged
- [ ] Any gap identified during the Axis evidence audit process

## Evidence note
"Insufficiently evidenced" ≠ "unsupported." Document this distinction explicitly for any claim Axis makes in the app where the evidence is absent or mixed rather than negative.
