# 09 — Systematic Reviews

## Scope
Systematic reviews (SRs) across all domains covered in 01–08 and 11–14. Kept separate to maintain visibility of the evidence hierarchy.

## What belongs here
- Peer-reviewed systematic reviews (with or without meta-analysis) on any topic relevant to the Axis library
- Scoping reviews with a systematic search strategy
- Systematic reviews of reviews (umbrella reviews) on female physiology and exercise

## What does NOT belong here
- Narrative reviews without a systematic search (→ file in the relevant domain folder 01–08)
- Meta-analyses (→ 10, though an SR+MA lives in *both* 09 and 10)
- Individual primary studies

## Filing note
A paper that is both a systematic review and a meta-analysis should be filed in both 09 and 10. Use Zotero's multi-collection feature rather than duplicating the entry.

## Inclusion criteria
Must include a systematic search of at least two databases with stated inclusion/exclusion criteria and PRISMA (or equivalent) reporting. Record: databases searched, number of included studies, GRADE or equivalent quality rating if provided.

## Evidence note
SRs are high in the evidence hierarchy but are not infallible. Note if an SR's included studies predominantly use calendar-only phase verification — this limits the conclusions. A well-conducted SR of weak primary studies is still limited by those studies.
