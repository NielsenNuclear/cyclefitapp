# 12 — Wearables & Tracking

## Scope
Validity, accuracy, and user behavior related to cycle and symptom tracking methods, including wearable devices, apps, and manual methods.

## What belongs here
- Accuracy and reliability of wearable sensors for cycle tracking: BBT wristbands, heart rate variability, skin temperature (Ōura, Whoop, Apple Watch, Garmin)
- Validity of app-based cycle prediction algorithms against gold-standard hormone assays
- Adherence to self-monitoring and symptom logging: what drives consistent tracking behavior
- User accuracy in retrospective symptom reporting vs prospective real-time logging
- Comparison of cycle-phase verification methods: calendar vs LH strip vs BBT vs serum
- Privacy and data quality in consumer femtech tracking apps (where data quality is the subject)

## What does NOT belong here
- General mHealth engagement not specific to tracking (→ 11)
- Wearable performance monitoring in a training context without cycle dimension (→ 02)
- Regulation and privacy law for femtech (→ 13)
- Cycle mechanics themselves (→ 01)

## Inclusion criteria
Validation studies against a gold standard (serum LH or hormone assay) are highest priority. Observational studies of real-world tracking behavior accepted. Record: device/app tested, gold-standard comparison, sample size, sensitivity/specificity or agreement metric.
