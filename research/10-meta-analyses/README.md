# 10 — Meta-Analyses

## Scope
Quantitative meta-analyses synthesising effect sizes across primary studies. Top of the evidence hierarchy for Axis.

## What belongs here
- Meta-analyses on any topic within the Axis library scope (domains 01–08)
- Network meta-analyses comparing multiple phase-based interventions
- Individual participant data (IPD) meta-analyses

## What does NOT belong here
- Systematic reviews without quantitative synthesis (→ 09)
- Primary studies (→ relevant domain folder)

## Filing note
A combined SR + MA is filed in *both* 09 and 10.

## Inclusion criteria
Must report at least one pooled effect size (standardised mean difference, odds ratio, relative risk, or equivalent) with confidence interval. Record: number of included studies, total N, primary outcome, pooled effect size + CI, heterogeneity (I²), and any evidence of publication bias assessment.

## Evidence note
Heterogeneity (I²) is critical in this literature. High I² (> 75%) in a menstrual cycle meta-analysis often reflects methodological variation (phase verification, outcome measures, populations) rather than true effect variation — and should be flagged. A meta-analysis with I² = 90% tells you less than it might appear to.

## Key papers to track
- Kissow et al. (2022) — meta-analysis on cycle phase and performance, cited frequently; engage directly with its effect size estimates and heterogeneity figures
- Any BJSM or Sports Medicine meta-analyses on resistance training in women
