# 11 — App Design & Behavior Change

## Scope
Behavior change theory, adherence, engagement, and UX for health and fitness apps — the design science behind Axis.

## What belongs here
- Behavior change frameworks with evidence of efficacy in digital health: COM-B, Self-Determination Theory, Habit Loop, Implementation Intentions, Motivational Interviewing
- Adherence to exercise programs: predictors, barriers, and effective interventions
- mHealth and fitness app engagement: what drives long-term use vs dropout
- Gamification evidence in health apps (effectiveness, pitfalls, population moderators)
- Notification and nudge design: timing, framing, frequency, user control
- Personalization and adaptive systems: does adaptive > generic in health behavior?
- Dark patterns in health apps and their ethical/regulatory status
- User research methods applied to health apps: diary studies, EMA, experience sampling

## What does NOT belong here
- Technical implementation of apps (→ not in the research library)
- Wearable sensor validity (→ 12)
- Digital health regulation and privacy (→ 13)

## Inclusion criteria
Peer-reviewed studies, position statements, and high-quality grey literature (e.g. NHS digital health evidence standards). RCTs of mHealth interventions preferred; descriptive and qualitative studies accepted if they inform design decisions directly.
