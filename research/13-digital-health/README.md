# 13 — Digital Health

## Scope
Femtech, digital therapeutics, and research-relevant regulation, privacy, and ethics for health data apps.

## What belongs here
- Femtech market landscape, user demographics, and evidence base (or lack thereof)
- Digital therapeutics (DTx) regulatory frameworks: FDA, CE-marking, NHS evidence standards
- Health data privacy: HIPAA, GDPR, and their application to menstrual data specifically
- Post-Dobbs reproductive data privacy concerns and their implications for app design
- Algorithmic bias and health equity in AI/ML-based health tools
- Informed consent frameworks for health apps collecting sensitive data
- Evidence standards for making health claims in consumer apps
- Data ethics in femtech: commercialisation of menstrual data, third-party sharing

## What does NOT belong here
- App engagement and behavior change science (→ 11)
- Wearable sensor validity (→ 12)
- Clinical sports medicine (→ 08)

## Inclusion criteria
Policy documents, legal analyses, regulatory guidance, peer-reviewed ethics/law papers, and high-quality journalism with cited primary sources. Grey literature accepted if from a named regulatory body (FDA, ICO, EMA).

## Evidence note
This folder informs product and legal decisions, not clinical recommendations. Flag anything that has direct implications for how Axis describes its recommendations to users (avoiding "diagnose," "treat," "prescribe" language).
