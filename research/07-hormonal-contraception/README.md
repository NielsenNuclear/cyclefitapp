# 07 — Hormonal Contraception

## Scope
Effects of hormonal contraceptives (combined oral contraceptives, progestin-only pills, implants, injections, hormonal IUDs) on exercise performance, training adaptation, and recovery.

## What belongs here
- Studies comparing performance or adaptation outcomes between OCP users and naturally cycling women
- Effects of exogenous hormones on muscle protein synthesis, strength, and hypertrophy
- Hormonal contraception and cardiovascular/respiratory adaptations
- Effects on recovery, injury risk, and ligament laxity (ACL literature)
- Pill-phase vs non-pill-phase comparisons for OCP users
- Formulation differences (monophasic vs triphasic, progestin type, ethinyl estradiol dose) and their relevance to training

## What does NOT belong here
- Natural menstrual cycle physiology (→ 01)
- Non-hormonal contraception unless used as a control condition
- Clinical gynecology not relevant to athletic performance

## Inclusion criteria
Studies must specify the type and formulation of contraceptive used (OCP type, progestin generation). Record formulation, dosing phase, and comparison group for every entry.

## Evidence note
OCP users are often implicitly excluded from menstrual cycle research or lumped into "women" samples without disclosure. Flag any study in 01–05 that does not report OCP status of participants — this is a common confound.
