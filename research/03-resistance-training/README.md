# 03 — Resistance Training

## Scope
Strength and hypertrophy *adaptation* studies — multi-week training interventions, not single-session performance snapshots.

## What belongs here
- Randomised and non-randomised interventions comparing resistance training outcomes across cycle phases or between phase-timed and standard programming
- Muscle hypertrophy (lean mass, muscle cross-sectional area) outcomes in training studies
- Strength gains (1RM, multi-rep max) over weeks or months as a function of phase-based programming
- Studies on phase-timed periodisation (e.g. follicular-phase loading vs luteal-phase deload protocols)
- Hormonal influences on muscle protein synthesis, anabolism, and satellite cell activity in the context of training

## What does NOT belong here
- Single-session strength measures without a training intervention (→ 02)
- Endurance adaptation studies (→ 04)
- Systematic reviews of resistance training (→ 09) or meta-analyses (→ 10)

## Key papers to engage directly (do not sidestep)
- Wikström-Frisén et al. (2017) — phase-based training intervention, one of the strongest pro-phase papers
- Sung et al. (2014) — follicular-phase strength gains
- Counter-evidence: studies finding no significant phase × training interaction

## Inclusion criteria
Multi-week (≥ 4 weeks) resistance training interventions in women of reproductive age. Record: sample size, phase verification method, training protocol, outcome measures, and effect sizes.
