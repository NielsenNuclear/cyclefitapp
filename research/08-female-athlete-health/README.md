# 08 — Female Athlete Health

## Scope
RED-S (Relative Energy Deficiency in Sport), menstrual dysfunction, bone health, injury risk, and the Female Athlete Triad — health conditions specific to or overrepresented in female athletes.

## What belongs here
- RED-S: definition, prevalence, screening tools (LEAF-Q, etc.), clinical management
- The Female Athlete Triad: low energy availability, menstrual dysfunction, low bone mineral density
- Menstrual dysfunction in athletes: amenorrhea (primary and secondary), oligomenorrhea — prevalence, causes, and health consequences
- Bone stress injuries and bone mineral density in relation to menstrual status
- ACL and musculoskeletal injury risk across the cycle and in relation to hormonal status
- Iron deficiency and anemia in female athletes
- Cardiovascular health in female athletes (long-term adaptations, screening)
- IOC Consensus Statements on Female Athlete Health

## What does NOT belong here
- General sports nutrition (→ 06)
- Hormonal contraception as a treatment unless health outcomes are the focus (→ 07)
- Performance studies without a health outcome (→ 02)

## Priority sources
IOC 2014 and 2023 Consensus Statements, ACSM Female Athlete Triad Coalition Position Stand, British Journal of Sports Medicine RED-S papers.

## Inclusion criteria
Clinical studies, epidemiological studies, consensus statements, and position papers. Include grey literature (clinical guidelines) from major sports medicine bodies.
