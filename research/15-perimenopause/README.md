# 15 — Perimenopause

## Scope
Training, body composition, symptom management, and health outcomes for women in the perimenopausal and postmenopausal transition.

## What belongs here
- Resistance training and aerobic exercise interventions in peri- and postmenopausal women
- Body composition changes across the menopause transition and exercise responses
- Symptom management through exercise: vasomotor symptoms (hot flashes), sleep disruption, mood, joint pain
- Bone health, osteoporosis risk, and exercise-based prevention in this population
- Hormonal changes of the perimenopause transition (estrogen decline, FSH rise) and their implications for training adaptation
- Menopausal hormone therapy (MHT) interactions with exercise and training adaptation
- Comparisons of pre- vs peri- vs postmenopausal exercise responses (especially where the transition itself is the variable of interest)
- Practical programming guidance specific to this population

## What does NOT belong here
- Studies of reproductive-age eumenorrheic women (→ 01–08 as appropriate)
- OCP and hormonal contraceptive effects (→ 09)
- Clinical menopause management without an exercise component (out of scope for Axis entirely)

## Inclusion criteria
Any study whose primary population is perimenopausal or postmenopausal women, or that explicitly compares outcomes across menopause status as the primary variable. Minimum: controlled study design or consensus/position statement. Tier 3 books from qualified authors retained under `seminal-exception` tagging.

## Evidence note
The perimenopause evidence base is smaller and more heterogeneous than the reproductive-age literature. Sample sizes are often small. "No effect" findings are common and may reflect underpowering rather than a true null. Grade accordingly and flag where evidence base is thin.

Perimenopause studies often do not standardise against a menopause staging system (e.g. STRAW+10). Note staging method where reported; flag studies with vague stage definitions as **high misclassification risk**, analogous to the calendar-only phase-verification flag in folders 01–08.
